What is Computer Networking?

Networking is a group of computers of interconnected computers and devices that share information. By simply connecting to the internet, you've automatically joined the largest network in the world since the internet is just made up of a bunch of computers that share data. Networks can range in size from two devices up to the largest network in the world, the internet.

What are the Components of Networking?

  • Device - Can be a computer, tablet, mobile phone, gaming console, IoT device, and server.
  • Transmission Medium - Connects network devices.
  • Network Interface (NIC) - Converts the digital data into a signal that can be transmitted along the transmission medium.
  • Routers - Connect multiple networks.
  • Switches - Connect multiple computers inside of a network.
  • Access Points - Connect wireless network hosts.
  • Networking Protocols - Standards that define how data is formatted and how the network hosts will talk to each other.

What are the Benefits of Networking?

Networking is beneficial because it Consolidates (centralized) data storage, Shares peripheral devices such as printers, Increases internal and external communications, Increases productivity and collaboration, Provides a centralized management location for user accounts and security, and Allows a network technician to remotely troubleshoot issues.

Types of Networks:

  • Peer-to-Peer Networks
  • Cilent-Server Networks
  • Body Area Networks (BAN)
  • Personal Area Networks (PAN)
  • Local Area Networks (LAN)
  • Campus Area Networks (CAN)
  • Metropolitan Area Networks (MAN)
  • Wide Area Networks (WAN)
  • Storage Area Networks (SAN)
